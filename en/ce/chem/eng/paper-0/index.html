Chemistry - English version Chemistry - English version The Hong Kong Diploma of Secondary Education (HKDSE) Chemistry subject is a Category A elective within the Science Education learning area. It focuses on understanding the link between macroscopic chemical phenomena and the microscopic world of atoms, molecules, and ions. Students develop scientific and logical thinking skills, learn to apply chemical knowledge to problem-solving, and explore the social, economic, and environmental impact of chemistry.

Key aspects of the DSE Chemistry subject:

Curriculum:

The curriculum covers topics like the microscopic world, metals, acids and bases, fossil fuels, and chemical reactions.

Assessment:

Students are assessed on their ability to recall and understand chemical facts, use apparatus, conduct experiments, analyze data, and apply their knowledge.

Practical Skills:

The subject emphasizes hands-on laboratory work, including using apparatus, performing experiments, and making accurate observations.

Problem-Solving:

Students learn to analyze information, interpret data, and solve problems related to chemistry.

Real-World Applications:

The curriculum explores the relevance of chemistry to daily life, society, and the environment.

Scientific Attitudes:

Students are encouraged to develop positive values and attitudes towards science and lifelong learning.

In essence, the DSE Chemistry subject aims to equip students with a strong foundation in chemistry, develop their scientific thinking skills, and prepare them for further studies or careers in related fields.
