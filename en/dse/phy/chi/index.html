Physics - 中文卷 The Hong Kong Diploma of Secondary Education (HKDSE) Physics curriculum aims to develop students’ understanding of fundamental physics concepts, their ability to apply these concepts to solve problems, and their awareness of the applications and implications of physics. The curriculum covers a broad range of topics, including mechanics, thermal physics, waves, electricity and magnetism, and nuclear physics. Students are also expected to develop practical skills through laboratory work and investigative studies.

Key components of the HKDSE Physics curriculum include:

  • Core Topics:
    The core curriculum covers fundamental physics concepts and principles across various areas such as mechanics, thermal physics, waves, electricity and magnetism, and nuclear physics.
  • Elective Modules:
    Students choose one or two elective modules from a selection including astronomy, atomic world, energy, and medical physics.
  • Practical Work:
    Students engage in laboratory work, including experiments and investigative studies, to develop their practical skills and understanding of scientific methodology.
  • Assessment:
    The HKDSE Physics examination assesses students’ knowledge and understanding of the curriculum, their ability to apply physics concepts, and their practical skills.

Assessment objectives of HKDSE Physics examination:

  • Recalling and understanding physics facts, concepts, and principles.
  • Applying knowledge to explain phenomena and solve problems.
  • Understanding the use of apparatus and experimental methods.
  • Presenting, analyzing, and interpreting data.
  • Communicating information clearly and logically.
  • Understanding the applications of physics and its implications.
  • Evaluating physics-related issues.

Skills Developed:

Through the HKDSE Physics curriculum, students develop a range of skills, including:

  • Knowledge and Understanding: Acquiring a solid foundation in physics concepts and principles.
  • Problem-Solving: Applying knowledge to solve physics problems.
  • Practical Skills: Conducting experiments, making measurements, and analyzing data.
  • Communication Skills: Presenting scientific information clearly and effectively.
  • Critical Thinking: Evaluating physics-related issues and making informed judgments.

The HKDSE Physics curriculum is designed to equip students with a strong foundation in physics and related skills, preparing them for further studies or careers in science and technology.
